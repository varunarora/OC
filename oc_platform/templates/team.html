Varun Arora, Founding Director

Varun Arora works on product and curriculum. He holds a Bachelors and Masters in Information Systems Management from Carnegie Mellon University. Previously, Varun has worked for Inkling, United Nations, Ooredoo, and a controversial One Laptop Per Child deployment in Polynesia.

Varun grew up in Oman building websites and apps for learning and education communities. In his days as a student in higher ed, he immersed himself in learning theory, curriculum and existing education technologies, and even taught a semester-long university class. In 2013, the International Telecommunications Union named him a 'Young Innovator', and he had been previously named a Zeitgeist Young Mind by Google and Livity. He has been a Lingren Fellow and recently ended his Anne Lewis Social Innovation Fellowship.

Varun loves fine chocolate, scented candles, and cooking vegetarian food.

Joshua Siktar, Lead Content Developer

Joshua Siktar works on authentic learning content and community. Currently a senior, Joshua spends an enormous amount of effort in contributing to and building communities around open-source mathematics and chemistry learning content, and has recently started authoring engaging real-world activities for the teacher community. He also is responsible for curating a lot of the content on the website. Joshua started as an intern in January 2013. Joshua is also a leading contributor (on student perspectives in teaching) to OpenCurriculum's blog.

Based in Pittsburgh, Joshua's high school years at Winchester Thurston High School revolved around his advancing commununity interest and knowledge in math, chemistry and other subjects, apart from pursuing his musical passion. He has been an aspiring teacher for as long as he can remember. He has also recently begun programming and hopes to become much better at it!

Joshua loves to talk about his research in understanding investments.

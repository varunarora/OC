Our story (for your reading pleasure)

Our founder, Varun, spent a huge amount of time in middle and high school building social websites hosting learning content and building communities for his school. In 2009, as an undergraduate student at Carnegie Mellon University in Qatar, when he got the opportunity to spend 3 months on assessing a One Laptop Per Child deployment in the island country Niue, he finally got the chance to apply his learnings in a developing country. What he learned there would change the course of his life and become the reasons that gave birth to OpenCurriculum.

Varun spent the rest of his undergraduate and graduate degrees focusing on learning about technology and international development, education & literacy, software engineering, information design and online communities. He started working on OpenCurriculum as a proof-of-concept in mid-2010 and began development of a web platform in mid-2011 after putting together a team. By mid-2012, OpenCurriculum would go on to be released in private beta and fail magnanimously quietly. Varun wasn’t done yet.

While working on OpenCurriculum’s v 1.0, Varun became friends with Zeinab, his neighbor at Shirley Apartments who would invite him to free food events all around campus. Zeinab started contributing content early on, and went on to lead content efforts since the beginning of 2013, which is when OpenCurriculum got its new beginning.

While the team got a couple of micro-grants in the past to pay for basic costs, the project has never received any real funding.

OpenCurriculum is a non-profit technology initiative to empower people to create and share K-12 curricula material around the world. Started in 2010, it aims to create the largest repository of openly-licensed textbook content that can be used to print and distribute millions of free textbooks in the developing world. To get access to the amazing content catalog, or to contribute your own feedback on a K-12 curriculum you care about, visit www.theopencurriculum.org
